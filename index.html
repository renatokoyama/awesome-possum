Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Trichosurus vulpecula
  • Size: 35-55 cm body length, plus 25-40 cm tail
  • Weight: 1.5-4.5 kg
  • Lifespan: 6-13 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Least Concern in Australia
  • Unique Feature: Bushy, prehensile tail that aids in climbing

Possum in Tree Hollow

Description

A brushtail possum peering out from its favorite eucalyptus tree den. These tree hollows provide essential shelter and are the preferred nesting sites for wild possums. Note the distinctive pointed ears and bright, curious eyes characteristic of the species.

Tree hollows are critical habitat features for brushtail possums. These natural cavities, which take 100-200 years to form in old-growth eucalyptus trees, offer protection from weather and predators. A single possum may maintain 2-5 different den sites within its territory and rotate between them every few days.

Nocturnal Feeding

Description

Captured during nighttime foraging, this image shows a brushtail possum eating eucalyptus leaves. Their specialized digestive system allows them to process the toxic compounds found in eucalyptus foliage. The photo highlights their dexterous front paws used for grasping food.

Brushtail possums are primarily nocturnal feeders, emerging shortly after sunset to forage. They spend 4-6 hours each night feeding, consuming approximately 200-300 grams of vegetation. Their enlarged cecum contains beneficial bacteria that help break down cellulose and neutralize the toxins in eucalyptus leaves.

Mother with Joey

Description

A touching moment showing a mother possum with her joey clinging to her back. Young possums spend several months with their mothers after leaving the pouch, learning essential survival skills. This bond is crucial for the joey's development and eventual independence.

After spending 4.5-5 months developing in the mother's pouch, joeys begin the "back-riding" phase. During this time (6-9 months of age), the joey clings to its mother's back during nightly foraging trips, learning territory boundaries, food selection, and predator avoidance. This extended parental care is essential for the joey's survival when it becomes independent.

Urban Adaptation

Description

A brushtail possum navigating a residential rooftop at dusk. This image illustrates the species' remarkable ability to thrive in urban environments. Their adaptability has made them common visitors to Australian suburbs, where they've learned to coexist with human populations.

Urban brushtail possums demonstrate remarkable behavioral flexibility. They've learned to navigate power lines, use building structures as den sites, access ornamental plants for food, and time their movements to avoid traffic and domestic pets. Urban populations often have higher survival rates due to reduced predation pressure and consistent food sources, though they face unique risks from vehicles and human-wildlife conflict.

The Famous Bushy Tail

Description

Close-up detail of the brushtail possum's distinctive bushy, prehensile tail. This remarkable appendage serves multiple functions: balance during climbing, gripping branches, and even as a signal during social interactions. The tail's thick fur helps distinguish this species from other possum varieties.

The brushtail possum's tail is truly remarkable, measuring 25-40 cm in length (nearly as long as the body). The tail is prehensile, meaning it can grasp and hold onto branches like a fifth limb. The underside of the tail tip has a naked, rough patch that provides enhanced grip. The bushy appearance comes from thick fur, especially at the base, which also serves as a fat storage site and helps with thermoregulation.

Grooming Behavior

Description

A possum engaged in self-grooming, an essential daily activity. Brushtail possums are fastidious about cleanliness, spending considerable time maintaining their fur. This behavior helps remove parasites and keeps their coat in optimal condition for insulation and water resistance.

Grooming is a critical behavior for brushtail possums. They spend significant time each day using their teeth and claws to clean their fur, remove debris, and eliminate parasites. Upon returning to their den after nightly foraging, possums engage in thorough self-grooming sessions, paying special attention to their face, ears, and paws. Mothers also groom their joeys, strengthening the maternal bond while keeping the young possum clean.

Dietary Habits of the Brushtail Possum

The Australian brushtail possum is primarily herbivorous, with a diverse diet that varies depending on habitat, season, and availability of food sources.

Primary Food Sources

🌿 Eucalyptus Leaves

The foundation of the brushtail possum's diet consists of eucalyptus leaves. These leaves contain toxic compounds that would be harmful to most animals, but possums have evolved specialized digestive systems to break down these toxins.

🍃 Native Australian Plants

In addition to eucalyptus, brushtail possums consume:

  • Acacia leaves and flowers
  • Native fruits and berries
  • Blossoms and flowers
  • Bark and tree sap
  • Seed pods and native grasses

🏙️ Urban Food Sources

  • Garden vegetables (especially roses, fruit trees)
  • Ornamental flowers and shrubs
  • Cultivated fruits (apples, pears, stone fruits)

Feeding Patterns and Frequency

Aspect Details
Feeding Time Primarily nocturnal; feeding from dusk until dawn
Daily Intake Approximately 200-300 grams of vegetation per night
Feeding Duration Typically 4-6 hours throughout the night
Water Requirements Obtains most moisture from food; drinks when available

⚠️ Important Note

Wildlife experts discourage feeding wild possums. Human food can be unhealthy and may encourage dependency, conflict, and disease transmission.

Mating and Reproduction

The reproductive cycle of the Australian brushtail possum is a fascinating example of marsupial biology.

🦘 What is the Gestational Period?

The gestation period for brushtail possums is remarkably short: only 16-18 days. This brief pregnancy is typical of marsupials. However, the joey continues development for many months in the mother's pouch.

Birth and Development Timeline

Stage Timing Description
Gestation 16-18 days Pregnancy period before birth
Birth Day 0 Joey weighs only 0.2 grams
In-Pouch 0-4.5 months Joey permanently attached to teat
First Emergence ~4.5-5 months Joey begins leaving pouch briefly
Back-Riding ~6-7 months Joey rides on mother's back
Weaning ~7-9 months Joey becomes independent

👶 How Many Offspring?

Brushtail possums typically give birth to only ONE joey at a time. While the mother has four teats in her pouch, she almost always produces a single offspring per breeding cycle. Twins are extremely rare.

🏠 How Long with Parents?

Young possums stay with their mother for 7-9 months total, from birth through weaning. After weaning, juveniles usually disperse within a few weeks to months. By 12-14 months, most are fully independent.

Parental Care

  • Mother provides intensive care throughout development
  • Joey learns foraging skills during back-riding phase
  • Mothers are highly attentive and protective
  • Young learn territory boundaries and food selection

Animal Habitat

Geographic Distribution

🗺️ Where on Earth Are They Found?

  • Native Range - Australia: Eastern Australia from Queensland through NSW, Victoria, and South Australia; Western Australia southwest; Tasmania
  • Introduced: New Zealand (1850s, now invasive)
  • Found from sea level to 1,200 meters elevation

Natural Habitat Requirements

🌿 What Natural Surroundings Do They Need?

Requirement Details
Tree Cover Eucalyptus forests ideal; also rainforests and open woodland
Denning Sites Tree hollows essential; also caves, rock crevices, human structures
Food Sources Access to eucalyptus or suitable vegetation
Territory Size Males: 1-5 hectares; Females: 0.5-2 hectares

Den Locations

🏠 Where Do They Make Their Home?

🌳 In Trees (Primary Choice)

Tree hollows are the preferred denning site:

  • Created by age, decay, or fire in large eucalyptus trees
  • Height: Typically 3-15 meters above ground
  • Provide protection from weather and predators
  • Possums often have 2-5 dens and rotate between them

⚠️ Hollow Scarcity

Natural tree hollows take 100-200 years to form. Loss of old-growth trees has created a shortage of natural dens, making this a conservation concern.

🏘️ In Urban Structures

  • Building roof spaces and ceiling cavities
  • Wall cavities in houses and sheds
  • Chimneys (if uncapped)
  • Under decks and porches

🪨 In Rocky Areas

  • Rock crevices and caves
  • Hollow logs on the ground
  • Dense vegetation thickets

Anatomy and Biology

Physical Dimensions

Total Length 60-95 cm 56-88 cm

Unique Physical Characteristics

The Distinctive Bushy Tail

  • Length: 25-40 cm, prehensile
  • Used as a "fifth limb" when climbing
  • Thickly furred and bushy
  • Functions: balance, gripping, fat storage, communication

🦘 Marsupial Pouch

Female possums have a forward-opening pouch:

  • Contains four teats
  • Opens toward the head (unusual)
  • Strong sphincter muscles close opening

Fur and Coloration

  • Highly variable: gray, brown, red-brown, black, or cream
  • Lighter belly (cream or white)
  • Dense, soft underfur with longer guard hairs

Internal Systems

Respiratory System

How the Animal Breathes:

  • Paired lungs with diaphragmatic breathing
  • 20-30 breaths per minute at rest
  • Efficient oxygen exchange for active lifestyle

Digestive System

  • Greatly enlarged cecum with bacteria
  • Bacteria break down cellulose and detoxify eucalyptus compounds
  • Extended colon for fermentation

Lifespan

⏱️ How Long Do They Live?

Environment Average Maximum
Wild 6-7 years 13 years
Urban 7-10 years 15 years
Captivity 10-13 years 17 years

🏙️ Urban Advantage

Urban possums often live longer due to reduced predation, consistent food, and secure den sites. However, they face vehicle collisions and domestic pets.

Animal Behavior

Activity Patterns

Nocturnal Lifestyle

Brushtail possums are strictly nocturnal:

  • Emerge shortly after sunset
  • Peak activity first 2-3 hours after dark
  • Return before sunrise
  • Sleep 14-18 hours during day

Foraging Behavior

🍃 How the Animal Gets Food

Arboreal Foraging

  • Use claws and prehensile tail to climb
  • Carefully smell and taste leaves before eating
  • Hold food with front paws while eating
  • Select younger, less toxic foliage
  • Reach out to blossoms for nectar

Ground Foraging

  • Descend for fallen fruits or low vegetation
  • More vigilant due to predation risk
  • Quick to climb to safety

Defensive Behaviors

🛡️ How They Protect Themselves

Primary Defense: Avoidance

  • Nocturnal activity reduces encounters with daytime predators
  • Arboreal lifestyle escapes ground predators
  • Den security in tree hollows during day
  • Vigilance with frequent pauses to scan

Active Defense

  • Rapid climbing to escape
  • Loud hisses and screeches to startle threats
  • Threat display with open mouth showing teeth
  • Biting and scratching if cornered
  • Scent release when extremely stressed

⚠️ When Threatened

A cornered brushtail possum can be surprisingly fierce. They'll stand their ground with gaping mouth, emit threatening hisses, and won't hesitate to bite. Never attempt to handle wild possums.

Sleep Behavior

😴 Sleeping Habits

Den Usage

  • Have a preferred main den
  • Maintain 2-5 den sites and rotate
  • Change dens every few days to weeks
  • Curl into ball with tail wrapped around body

Sleep Patterns

  • Sleep 14-18 hours per day
  • Deep sleep during midday
  • Light sleep near dawn and dusk
  • Can be roused quickly by disturbances
  • May enter torpor in extreme cold

Communication

Vocalizations

  • Hisses and screeches: Aggressive or alarmed
  • Chattering: During disputes
  • Grunts: Contact calls
  • Soft clicks: Mother-joey communication
  • Loud scream: Territorial advertisement

Scent Marking

  • Rub chest gland on branches and objects
  • Chin marking on surfaces
  • Advertise presence and territory ownership

Intelligence and Learning

  • Spatial memory: Remember complex routes
  • Problem solving: Can open latches and containers
  • Urban adaptation: Quickly learn human environments
  • Habituation: Reduced fear over time

🏙️ Urban Adaptations

Urban possums show remarkable flexibility. They've learned to navigate power lines, recognize feeding schedules, tolerate close proximity to people, and time movements to avoid traffic.

🤔 Did You Know? Possum Mix-Ups!
🎭 "Playing Possum" is actually about opossums!

You might have heard the phrase "playing possum" — it means pretending to be asleep or not there. But this actually describes the North American opossum, not the Australian possum! When opossums get very scared, their body automatically freezes and plays dead. Australian possums don't do this at all. The mix-up happened because both animals got called "possum," but they are different animals living on different sides of the world!

🧊 Crash and Eddie in Ice Age are really opossums!

In the Ice Age movies, the funny brothers Crash and Eddie are actually North American opossums — but lots of people (and even some adverts!) call them "possums." They're two different animals! Opossums live in America, while possums live in Australia. Easy to mix up, but now you know the difference! 🌏

🦊 Kylie in Fantastic Mr. Fox is in the wrong country!

Kylie the opossum in Fantastic Mr. Fox is a North American opossum, but the story takes place in the English countryside. Opossums don't actually live in England — so Kylie is a fun character who is a little bit in the wrong place! 😄
